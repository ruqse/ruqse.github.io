About Me

Hi there! I'm a scientist with a PhD in Biomedical Science, and I enjoy using computers to solve biological puzzles. Right now, I'm working at SciLifeLab, where I'm diving into the fascinating world of women's health. My current project is all about understanding the tiny organisms that live in the vagina - yes, you read that right! These little critters play a big role in keeping women healthy. I'm using clever computer programs to figure out which combinations of these microbes are linked to good health, and which might cause problems. I'm also playing detective with these microbes' family trees. By looking at their genetic blueprints, I'm piecing together how they're related to each other and where they came from. It's like solving a million-year-old mystery! This work is super exciting because it could help us develop better ways to keep women healthy and treat problems when they pop up. Who knew such tiny organisms could be so important?
